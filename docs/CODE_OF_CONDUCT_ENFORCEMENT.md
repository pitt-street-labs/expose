# Code of Conduct Enforcement Procedures

This document describes how [CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md](../CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md) violations are handled within the EXPOSE project.

## Scope

These procedures apply wherever the Code of Conduct applies:

- The GitHub repository: issues, pull requests, code review, discussions, and wiki.
- Official communication channels (mailing lists, chat platforms).
- Project events: conferences, meetups, and talks given on behalf of EXPOSE.
- Public representations of the project by individuals acting in an official capacity.

## Reporting a violation

- **General reports:** Email **conduct@korlogos.com**. Include a description of the behavior, links or screenshots if available, and any relevant context.
- **Sensitive reports** (e.g., involving a maintainer, or where confidentiality is critical): Email **security@korlogos.com**. These reports are handled by the project lead directly.
- Reports may also be filed privately via GitHub by contacting the project lead.

All reporters are protected by the confidentiality provisions below.

## Response process

| Step | Timeline | Action |
|------|----------|--------|
| **Acknowledgment** | Within 48 hours | Reporter receives confirmation that the report was received. |
| **Investigation** | Within 7 days | Maintainers review the report, gather context from relevant venues, and interview parties if needed. The reporter is not required to interact with the respondent. |
| **Decision** | Within 14 days | Maintainers determine the appropriate enforcement level (see ladder below) and document the rationale. |
| **Communication** | Within 2 business days of decision | The reporter is informed of the outcome. The respondent is informed of the decision and its basis. |

Complex cases may exceed these timelines. If so, the reporter receives status updates at least every 7 days.

## Enforcement ladder

Responses are graduated based on severity, pattern, and impact.

| Level | Action | Duration | Typical triggers |
|-------|--------|----------|-----------------|
| **1 -- Private warning** | Private message clarifying expectations and the specific violation. | N/A | First-time minor violations, unintentional breaches of community norms. |
| **2 -- Public warning + restriction** | Public moderation note in the relevant venue. Temporary loss of interaction privileges (commenting, PR submission). | 7 days | Repeated minor violations, or a single moderate violation after a Level 1 warning. |
| **3 -- Temporary ban** | Removal from all project spaces. | 30 days | Serious violations, sustained patterns despite prior warnings, or hostile/threatening behavior. |
| **4 -- Permanent ban** | Permanent removal from all project spaces. | Indefinite | Egregious violations (harassment, threats, doxxing), or continued violations after a Level 3 ban. |

Each enforcement action includes a written rationale shared with the respondent.

## Appeals

A respondent may appeal any enforcement decision by emailing **conduct@korlogos.com** within 14 days of notification. Appeals are reviewed by maintainers who were not involved in the original decision. The appeal review is completed within 14 days. The appeal decision is final.

## Confidentiality

- The identity of reporters is never disclosed to the respondent or the public without the reporter's explicit written consent.
- Enforcement records are retained internally but are not published with identifying details.
- The annual transparency report (see CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md) includes aggregate statistics only.

## Maintainer accountability

Maintainers are subject to the same enforcement ladder as community members. Reports involving a maintainer are handled by the project lead or by uninvolved maintainers, as described in the sensitive-reports path above.
